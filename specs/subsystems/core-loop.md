# Core Loop

## The Game in One Sentence

Manage a party of four adventurers through dungeon expeditions — draft your team, kick down doors, watch them fight absurd monsters, and decide how far to push your luck before bailing or dying.

## Core Tension

**Greed vs. Survival.** Every room deeper is better rewards but more risk. Your characters accumulate damage, curses stack, resources dwindle. The party member you've invested in most is the one you're most afraid to lose. Complete the dungeon for the big payout — or bail early and keep whatever you grabbed.

## Player Experience at Three Time Scales

### Fight-to-Fight (each room)

1. **Choose a door** — rooms are revealed as you explore. Most are hidden (could be anything), some are visible (rest area, treasure room).
2. **Watch the battle play out** — auto-battle. Characters and monsters take turns based on initiative. Each character has an attack pattern determined by their race, abilities, and equipped items.
3. **Deal with the aftermath** — loot drops, character deaths, curse effects. Rearrange equipment if needed. Decide: next room or bail?

### Expedition-to-Expedition (each dungeon run)

1. **Choose a quest** — 3 quests offered per run (1 on the first run), each with a dungeon attached. Dungeons are LLM-generated with randomized combinations of length (normal/long), difficulty (easy/medium/hard), and biome (cave, crypt, forest, tower). Biome determines monster types. No duplicate length+difficulty pairings. Each quest shows its reward upfront (gold, gear, or both) and has LLM-generated lore that hints at difficulty without labeling it directly. Pick the risk level you can handle.
2. **Assemble your party** — pick 4 from your roster. Equip them. Buy consumables from the shop (3 slots: healing potions and/or curse negators). Decide who's ready and who's sitting out.
3. **Explore the dungeon** — room by room, making push-your-luck decisions. Manage health, resources, and curse buildup.
4. **Cash out or complete** — bail at any point and keep all loot collected so far, but forfeit the quest reward and risk a random curse on a party member. Push to the end for the quest reward on top of your loot. Dead characters and their gear are gone forever.
5. **Return to base** — full free heal for all survivors. Buy one recruit at the tavern (max one per run). Buy consumables for next run. Mourn the dead. Pick the next quest.

### Run-to-Run (across the campaign)

1. **First run** — one dungeon available, weak party, scraping by. Every recruit feels precious.
2. **Escalation** — 3 quests per run, randomly generated with varying length/difficulty/biome combos, no duplicate pairings. Recruiting catches you up to where you need to be.
3. **The long grind** — dungeons keep getting harder. Your best characters are irreplaceable. One bad run can set you back hours. There is no finish line. Even if your entire roster dies, the game goes on — you recruit from scratch and rebuild.

## What the Player Actually Does

- **Before a dungeon**: Choose quest → select party → equip gear → buy consumables (healing + curse negators, 3 slots)
- **During a dungeon**: Choose which door to open → watch auto-battle → manage loot → decide to continue or retreat (bail = keep loot, no quest reward, possible curse)
- **After a dungeon**: Full heal → recruit one new character (max 1/run) → buy consumables → plan next run

## Win Condition

None. No endgame, no final boss, no victory screen, no game over. The game keeps generating harder dungeons and stronger monsters. If your entire roster dies, you recruit fresh level 1s and start rebuilding. The goal is to see how far you can push. Pure roguelike — your "score" is how far you got before it all fell apart.

## What Makes a "Good Move" Feel Good

- **The bail-out that saves your best character** — everyone groans when you retreat with 3 rooms left, but two runs later that character is still alive and carrying the team.
- **The perfect item-character combo** — finding the exact gear that makes a build click. "The +3 initiative boots on the rogue means she always goes first now."
- **The clutch auto-battle** — watching your underleveled party somehow scrape through a fight they shouldn't have won because positioning and initiative lined up perfectly.
- **The risky room that pays off** — opening a mystery door when your party is half-dead and finding a treasure room instead of a boss.
