# Sprite Generation Prompts — Munchkin Dungeon

Art style for ALL prompts: Cartoonish and slightly hyperrealistic in the style of Ren & Stimpy — exaggerated anatomy with grotesque close-up-worthy details, bulging bloodshot eyes, visible veins and muscle fibers, glistening textures on skin and materials, dramatic over-the-top poses. Hand-drawn cartoon illustration feel, NOT pixel art. Characters should look like they belong in a 90s gross-out cartoon that takes itself way too seriously. Transparent background on all character sprites.

**Monster theme**: All monsters are hedgehog/porcupine mutations (PostHog's mascot is a hedgehog named Max). Think spiny, quilly, round-bodied creatures — not pigs. The base creature is always recognizably a hedgehog or porcupine, then mutated/corrupted into the monster variant.

## Heroes

### 1. Warrior

A stocky, barrel-chested human warrior in a dramatic battle stance, gripping a dented iron sword and a shield covered in scratches and what might be dried soup. Heavy plate armor that's slightly too tight — belly bulging between the seams, rivets straining. Sweat beads the size of grapes rolling down a thick forehead with one massive vein pulsing visibly. Square jaw clenched so hard you can see every muscle fiber in the neck. Eyes wide with unhinged determination, pupils tiny. Blue steel armor with scratched gold trim. Scuffed boots planted wide. The sword has a slightly bent tip. Everything about this person screams "I will protect you, but I will complain about it loudly." Front-facing, full body, transparent background.

### 2. Mage

A lanky, slightly hunched mage in billowing purple robes that are two sizes too big, sleeves dragging on the ground. Gripping a gnarled wooden staff topped with a crystal orb that's cracked and held together with tape and hope — it still glows an ominous purple. Wild, unkempt hair shooting in every direction like they just stuck a fork in a socket. Enormous bags under wild, bloodshot eyes with irises that spiral slightly. Bony fingers with knobby knuckles wrapped around the staff, fingernails slightly too long. A thin mustache that's trying its absolute best. Arcane symbols float lazily around them, one of them is clearly just a doodle of a cat. The robe has suspicious burn marks and a coffee stain. Full body, front-facing, transparent background.

### 3. Rogue

A wiry, grinning rogue in a hooded leather outfit, crouched in a ready stance with two curved daggers that gleam with an oily sheen. The hood casts a shadow but you can still see that self-satisfied smirk — one gold tooth catching the light. Lean muscles visible through tight-fitting dark green leather armor with too many buckles and pouches (one is clearly stuffed with snacks). Eyes narrowed to calculating slits with dilated pupils. A thin scar across the nose that they clearly think makes them look cool. Fingers are nimble and long, wrapped in dark hand wraps. Multiple hidden knife handles peek out from boots, belt, and sleeve. The whole pose says "I already stole your wallet and you haven't noticed yet." Full body, front-facing, transparent background.

### 4. Cleric

A broad, serene cleric in white and gold vestments that practically glow with divine energy — or maybe it's just really clean fabric in a dungeon, which is suspicious in itself. One hand raised holding a golden holy symbol that radiates warm light, the other clutching a thick prayer book with dozens of sticky-note bookmarks poking out. Round, kindly face with rosy cheeks and a calm smile, but the eyes — the eyes have seen things. Deep, knowing eyes with tiny reflections of flames in them. A neatly trimmed beard with a few grey streaks. Slight double chin. The vestments have intricate gold embroidery depicting scenes that, upon close inspection, are just recipes. A heavy golden mace hangs from the belt, well-used. Radiates an aura of "I will heal you, but I will judge you." Full body, front-facing, transparent background.

## Tier 1 Monsters — Hedgehog Mutations (Chumps)

### 5. Spiny Hoglet

A tiny, shrieking baby hedgehog the size of a football, standing on its stubby hind legs in a comically aggressive battle stance. Oversized quills sticking out in every direction like an angry pincushion having a bad hair day. Beady, furious red eyes bulging out of a scrunched-up pointy face. The body is a round brown-tan ball with stubby legs, each tiny paw clenched into fists. Quills standing straight up on its back, vibrating with rage. A tiny pink nose twitching aggressively. Drool strands hanging from its snarling mouth full of tiny sharp teeth. This creature is 90% attitude and 10% hedgehog. Transparent background.

### 6. Mudgehog

A fat, sluggish hedgehog monster absolutely caked in thick, dripping brown mud that fills the gaps between its spines. The mud is chunky — you can see pebbles, a bone, and what appears to be a lost sock embedded between the quills. Tiny, nearly buried beady eyes peer out from the mud-caked face with a look of dull menace. Stubby legs barely visible under the blob-like spiny body, each step leaving a gooey puddle. The little snout pokes out like a periscope, nostrils twitching with each wheezing breath. Quills poking through the mud layer at odd angles, some bent, some encrusted with dried earth. The whole creature glistens wetly. It looks like it smells exactly as bad as you'd imagine. Transparent background.

### 7. Winged Quillhog

A medium-sized hedgehog hovering awkwardly in the air on absurdly tiny bat wings that shouldn't possibly support its spiny weight — the wings are flapping frantically, a blur of motion, veins visible through the translucent membrane. The hedgehog's face shows the strain: eyes bugging out, teeth gritted, neck veins popping. Quills rattling and shedding with each desperate wingbeat. A round, heavy spiny belly dangles below, jiggling dangerously — quills occasionally falling like rain. Stubby legs dangle uselessly, occasionally kicking at nothing. The wings have little clawed tips that look like they were glued on as an afterthought. Despite everything, it maintains an expression of furious dignity. Transparent background.

### 8. Quill Spitter

A large porcupine-hedgehog hybrid bristling with sharp, barbed quills across its entire humped back, each one glistening with a suspicious yellowish fluid. Hunched forward in an aggressive stance, its pointed snout wrinkled back to show rows of tiny sharp teeth. The quills on its back are raised and fanned out like a peacock display of pain. Small, mean eyes squinting from beneath a heavy brow ridge covered in shorter spines. Muscular haunches suggest it can launch those quills with surprising force. Several quills are already missing from recent volleys, leaving raw red patches. Coarse brown and tan fur between the quill clusters. A short, spiky tail that rattles like a warning. The overall silhouette is an angry, weaponized pincushion. Transparent background.

### 9. Zombie Hedgehog

An undead hedgehog shambling forward with vacant, glowing green eyes in sunken sockets. Decaying grey-green skin stretched tight over a round body, quills falling off in clumps revealing rotting flesh beneath. The most striking feature: clusters of luminescent mushrooms and toadstools growing directly out of its back between the remaining spines — some are pulsating gently, releasing tiny visible spores. The snout twitches constantly, driven by instinct even in death. Some quills have been replaced entirely by fungal growths. Jaw hangs slightly open, revealing green-tinged teeth and a tongue that's turned into a small mushroom colony. Dirt and roots cling to its paws as if it recently dug itself out of the ground. A faint green miasma surrounds it. Transparent background.

### 10. Quill Slime

A gelatinous, translucent green blob that has molded itself into the approximate shape of a hedgehog — complete with wobbly spines made of hardened slime poking out in all directions, two tiny ear shapes, and a pointed snout. You can see through its body: suspended inside are half-digested items — a boot, some coins, a turnip, and what might be an actual hedgehog skeleton. Two darker green orbs serve as eyes, floating inside the head area, occasionally drifting apart comically before snapping back together. The slime-spines wobble and reform constantly. The surface ripples and jiggles with each movement, leaving a glistening trail. The overall shape is barely holding together, threatening to collapse into a puddle at any moment. Transparent background.

### 11. Ghost Hoglet

A spectral baby hedgehog floating several inches off the ground, its body a translucent white-blue that fades to transparency at the edges. Ghostly quills that shimmer and phase in and out of visibility. Huge, sad puppy-dog eyes that glow with an ethereal inner light — this ghost is simultaneously adorable and deeply unsettling. The body shimmers, sometimes showing a faint spiny skeletal structure within. Wisps of ectoplasm trail from its paws and quill tips like smoke. The tiny snout is slightly upturned, giving it a permanently surprised expression. Faint, ghostly sparkles surround it. Despite being a ghost, it still has the round chubbiness of a baby hedgehog — the afterlife hasn't changed its body shape. A small, barely visible halo sits crookedly above its quills. Transparent background.

### 12. Spikeling

A small but intensely aggressive hedgehog creature, curled into a semi-ball charging stance with quills fully extended like a living buzzsaw. The spines are oversized, chipped and scarred from constant use as weapons. Dark brown bristly quills stand on end across its entire body, some tipped with dried blood. Wild, bloodshot eyes with pinprick pupils locked onto its target with psychotic focus. A compact, muscular body built for rolling and ramming. Scratches cover its underbelly like battle scars. Steam visibly puffs from its tiny twitching nose. The paws dig into the ground, ready to launch into a deadly spin attack. Every single quill is tensed and pointed forward. This is a hedgehog that has chosen violence as a lifestyle. Transparent background.

## Tier 2 Monsters — Hedgehog Mutations (Troublemakers)

### 13. War Hedgehog

A massive, heavily armored battle hedgehog standing upright on thick hind legs. Crude but effective metal plates are bolted and strapped between its enormous quills — some riveted right through the spines, with angry red welts around each bolt. Chainmail drapes over its shoulders where quills are shorter. Red war paint streaked across its pointed snout in tribal patterns. Glowing red eyes burn with tactical intelligence beneath a crude iron helmet fitted around the quills. Carries a massive spiked shield in one paw (redundant, given the built-in spikes). Scars crisscross every visible patch of belly skin. The body is a wall of quills and metal, round and imposing. Sharpened quills protrude through the armor like natural bayonets. A tattered red banner is tied between its tallest back-spines. This hedgehog has seen war, and war blinked first. Transparent background.

### 14. Quill Troll

A towering, grotesque hybrid of troll and hedgehog — easily twice the height of any hero. Mottled green-brown skin stretched over enormous slabs of muscle, with massive quills the size of javelins protruding from its hunched back and shoulders. Tiny, stupid eyes sit deep in a heavy brow, showing just enough intelligence to be dangerous. Carries a club that's basically an uprooted tree with rocks still attached to the roots. Its round troll-belly is covered in short, stubby defensive spines. Warts and boils cover its skin between the quills, some disturbingly close to popping. Long, pointed ears droop down past its quill line. Drool cascades from its open mouth in glistening ropes. When it flexes, quills rattle ominously. Transparent background.

### 15. Spinomancer

A hedgehog standing on hind legs wearing an oversized, tattered purple wizard robe and a tall, bent pointed hat with a hole cut for the tallest quill to poke through. Clutching a gnarled staff topped with a floating orb of swirling purple energy — the orb occasionally takes the shape of a tiny screaming hedgehog face. Mystic runes and arcane symbols float in lazy circles around its quills, glowing with purple light. Intelligent, cunning beady eyes that glow faintly. A small pair of spectacles perched on the pointed snout. The robe has mysterious stains and star patterns, with small burn holes where quills have poked through the fabric. One paw holds the staff, the other is raised with sparkling energy gathering at the claw tips. A spell book floats nearby, pages flipping on their own, several pages impaled on stray quills. The whole ensemble says "I went to wizard school and I will not be disrespected." Transparent background.

### 16. Iron Hedgehog

A hulking mechanical-organic hedgehog golem — spiny flesh fused with riveted iron plates, gears, and pipes in a steampunk nightmare. Each quill has been replaced or augmented with a sharpened iron spike, creating a walking iron maiden. Glowing red eyes peer through a heavy iron faceplate bolted around its snout. Steam vents from its nostrils with each huffing breath, creating little clouds. Pistons and hydraulics are visible where legs meet the round body, hissing and clicking with each movement. The iron plating is rusted in patches, revealing raw skin underneath where flesh meets metal. A furnace-like glow emanates from seams between the mechanical quills. Oil and something that might be blood drip from its joints. The sound it makes is half squeak, half grinding metal. Transparent background.

### 17. Dire Quillbeast

An oversized, primal dire hedgehog — pure prehistoric aggression. Massive quills the size of swords fan out from its humped back, each one wickedly barbed and scarred from combat. The hide is thick, leathery dark brown, covered in old scars and fresh wounds between sparse remaining fur patches. Bloodshot, yellowed eyes with a predatory intelligence burn beneath a heavy, scarred brow. The body is enormous and round — like a boulder covered in spears. Muscles ripple visibly under the exposed belly skin with each heaving breath. The stance is low, weight forward, ready to roll through a stone wall without slowing down. Blood from a recent fight stains its longest quills. It can curl into a devastating ball of death at a moment's notice. This is an apex predator that happens to be a hedgehog. Transparent background.

### 18. Quill Wraith

A ghostly, skeletal hedgehog knight — spectral dark armor floating over a translucent, decaying spiny form. The armor is ornate but corroded, with a hedgehog-skull helmet visor and tattered dark cape that flows into pure shadow. Spectral quills phase in and out of existence, each one trailing purple ethereal smoke. Glowing purple eyes burn in deep sockets behind the visor. Wields a phantasmal greatsword that drips with dark ethereal energy, leaving purple afterimage trails. The body beneath the armor phases between visible and invisible — sometimes you see ghostly ribs, sometimes spectral quills, sometimes nothing. Ethereal wisps and dark particles swirl around it constantly. Ghost-quills occasionally detach and float in the air before dissolving. Its paws don't touch the ground — it hovers, leaving a trail of frost. An aura of dread surrounds it. Transparent background.

## Boss

### 19. Hogzilla, the Eternal Quill

An absolutely MASSIVE hedgehog monstrosity that fills the frame — a mountain of quills, fire, and fury. The body is the size of a small building, a colossal spiny sphere covered in thick, cracked hide like cooling lava — glowing orange-red fissures run between enormous quills that jut out like volcanic spires, each one pulsing with volcanic heat. Magma visibly bubbles and drips from the base of the largest quills. The quills themselves are obsidian-black with glowing orange veins running through them like molten rock. A crown of bleached bones and skulls sits impaled on the tallest head-quills — trophies from fallen adventurers. Eyes are pools of molten gold, burning with ancient rage and terrifying intelligence. The mouth, when open, reveals a furnace-like interior, lava dripping from rows of sharp teeth. Each massive paw cracks the ground beneath, leaving scorched prints. Smoke and embers constantly rise from between its quills like a living volcano. Despite the apocalyptic appearance, there's still something undeniably hedgehog-like about the round body and the tiny pointed snout. The ultimate boss. The final quill. Transparent background.

## Environment

### 20. Battle Background — Cave Dungeon

A wide-format dark dungeon cave interior viewed from a slight low angle. Rough, wet stone walls with moisture glistening on every surface. Flickering torches mounted in crude iron brackets cast dramatic, dancing orange light that creates deep shadows. The floor is uneven stone with suspicious puddles and cracks. Stalactites hang from the ceiling, some broken. Faint green bioluminescent moss grows in patches on the walls, adding an eerie secondary light source. Bones and abandoned equipment scattered in dark corners. The atmosphere is oppressive, damp, and dangerous — like the earth itself is hostile. Moody, dramatic lighting with strong contrast between warm torchlight and cold dark shadows. Wide format (roughly 3:1 ratio), suitable for a battle screen background.
